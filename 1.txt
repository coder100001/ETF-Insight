400 IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON AUTOMATIC CONTROL, VOL. 61, NO. 2, FEBRUARY 2016 Asymptotic Behavior of Recursive State Estimations With Intermittent Measurements Tong Zhou, Senior Member, IEEE Abstract —Convergence rate and stationary distribution approximation are investigated in this paper for both the covariancematrix of the Kalman ﬁlter and the pseudo-covariance matrix of arobust recursive state estimator developed by us. When the mea-surement dropping process is described by a Markov chain and anassociated plant is both controllable and observable, it is provedthat if the dropping probability is less than 1, these two matricesconverge exponentially to a stationary distribution independentof its initial values. Moreover, when they are initialized with thestabilizing solution of an associated algebraic Riccati equation,both of them are shown to converge to an ergodic process. Based onthese results, two approximations are derived for their stationaryprobability distributions, as well as a bound of approximationerrors. It has been made clear that rather than the delta functionsuggested by Kar and Moura, a series of delta functions give amore accurate approximation, and replacement of these estima-tors’ update gain by a constant matrix usually deteriorates theirsteady estimation accuracy. A numerical example is provided toillustrate the obtained theoretical results. Index Terms —Ergodicity, network system, random measurement dropping, recursive state estimation, stationary distribution. I. I NTRODUCTION WITH the development of network technologies, numerous novel anticipations, as well as various new technical issues, rise in system analysis and synthesis, due to the signiﬁcant differences in information exchange methodsbetween traditional and network systems. Transmission quality of a communication channel has signiﬁcant inﬂuences on the performance of a network system, as wireless link or utilization of commercial channels introduces many possibly detrimental factors, such as random data delay and/or loss, etc. These fac-tors may result in signiﬁcant system performance degradations, and even destroy system stability in the worst situation. To clarify and reduce inﬂuences of these factors, reinvestigationsare required for many system design methodologies. Among them, one important issue is state estimation with random measurement droppings, in which plant output measurementsare stochastically lost due to communication failures [2], [5], [19], [23]. Digital Object Identiﬁer 10.1109/TAC.2015.2434071Over the last decade, this problem has attracted extensive attention and various results have been obtained. On the basis of a separation principle, it is proved in [5] that for an optimalcontrol system, the intermediate nodes in state estimations do not need to access the plant control inputs, which enables the development of a recursive optimal state estimator. Some relations are established in [17] between a constant gain Kalman ﬁlter and switched estimators. A recursive state estimationprocedure is developed in [6], in which both the current and the previous plant output measurements are transmitted. In [19], optimality of the traditional Kalman ﬁlter is established underthe existence of random measurement droppings, provided that information is available in the received data on whether or not it is a measured plant output. It has also been made clear thatfor an unstable plant, to guarantee boundedness of the expectation of the covariance matrix of estimation errors (CMEE), in addition to controllability and observability, the measurementdropping probability must be lower than some threshold values. Some necessary and sufﬁcient conditions have been obtained for this boundedness of some particular classes of unstablesystems, which depend on the spectral radius of the plant state transition matrix (STM). It is observed, however, that although complete loss of plant output measurements usually has an essential zero probability, it is the dominating fact that leads to an inﬁnite expectation of this CMEE [16]. This observationmotivates investigations on its probability distribution, which is argued to be a more appropriate measure on the performance of a state estimator with random data missing [12], [16]. Particularly, using a Stieltjies transformation, some explicit formulas are obtained in [21] that enable computations of the steady eigenvalue distribution of the aforementioned CMEEfor a stable plant. Some upper and lower bounds are derived respectively in [15], [16] for the probability that this CMEE is smaller than a prescribed positive deﬁnite matrix (PDM).Under the condition that an unstable plant has a diagonalizable STM, [12] shows that if some controllability and observability conditions are satisﬁed, the trace of this CMEE decays ac-cording to a power law. Based on the contractive properties of Riccati recursions and convergence conditions on random iterated functions, this CMEE is proved in [2] to converge in general to a stationary distribution independent of its initial values. ENDZHOU: ASYMPTOTIC BEHAVIOR OF RECURSIVE STATE ESTIMATIONS WITH INTERMITTENT MEASUREMENTS 401 When a plant model is not accurate, which is the general situation in solving an engineering problem, recursive state estimations that are robust against modelling errors have also been extensively investigated, for example, [4], [8], [14], [18], [22], and the references therein. Some of these methods have alreadybeen extended to systems with an imperfect communication channel, such as the works in [13], [23]. Particularly, a recursive robust state estimator is derived in [23] penalizing sensitivity ofan innovation process to modelling errors, which reduces to the Kalman ﬁlter when the plant model is accurate. Moreover, some necessary and sufﬁcient conditions have also been establishedon the convergence of its pseudo-covariance matrix (PCM) to a stationary distribution. These investigations have made many important theoretical issues clear about state estimations with random measurement arrivals, and the obtained results appear greatly helpful in the analysis and synthesis of network systems. Some impor-tant issues of this estimation problem, however, still need further efforts. Among them, one essential problem is about the convergence rate of the CMEE in the Kalman ﬁltering or the PCM in the robust estimations, and a more accurate characterization of their stationary distributions. Note that noexplicit forms are given for this stationary distribution in [2], [23], and there are still no efﬁcient methods in determining the good rate function of [9] which is required in obtainingthe best parameter of the convergence rate. Similar to those of the conventional Kalman ﬁlter [8], [18], convergence rate of these random matrices describes inﬂuences of their initialvalues on estimation accuracies which are usually not accurate, and their stationary distribution directly connects to steady estimation accuracies and properties of the steady update gainmatrices. Settlement of this problem is essential in determining various important characteristics of a networked system, such as quality requirements on the communication channel,possibilities of replacing the Kalman ﬁlter or the robust state estimator with a time invariant observer, etc. The former is important in network system design, while the latter is very attractive in reducing computational costs of a state estimator. This paper reinvestigates properties of the stationary distribution of the CMEE of the Kalman ﬁlter and the PCM of the robust state estimator of [23]. The data missing process is assumed to be described by a Markov chain, which can includethe Bernoulli process adopted in [9] as a special case. On the basis of the Riemannian metric deﬁned in [1] for the space of PDMs and the central limit theorem established in [10] forMarkov chains, it is proved that when an associated plant is both controllable and observable, these matrices converge to a stationary distribution, provided that the data arrival probabilityis greater than zero. Rather than a power law, the convergence rate is proved to be exponential. It has also been shown that when these matrices are initialized with the stablilizing solutionof an associated algebraic Riccati equation, both of them are stationary and ergodic. From these results, two approximations are given for their stationary distributions, as well as theirconvergence rate to the actual value. It has been made clear that compared with the delta function suggested in [9], a series of delta functions are more appropriate in approximating these stationary distributions. Moreover, a simple replacementof the update gain of these state estimators with a constant matrix usually sacriﬁces steady estimation accuracy, which is signiﬁcantly different from the conventional Kalman ﬁlter. Compared with the results of [23], major contributions of this paper lie in its clear description of the convergence rate,and explicit formulas for two approximations of the stationary distribution that can be simply computed. The outline of this paper is as follows. At ﬁrst, in Section II, the problem formulation is provided, together with some preliminary results on Markov processes and Riccati recursions. Afterwards, stationarity and ergodicity properties of theCMEE/PCM process are investigated in Section III, while Section IV derives an approximation of the stationary distribution of this CMEE/PCM, as well as its convergence rate tothe actual value. A numerical example is provided in Section V to illustrate the effectiveness and accuracy of the suggested approximation methods. Finally, some concluding remarks aregiven in Section VI. C ONCLUSION In this paper, asymptotic properties are investigated for both the CMEE of the Kalman ﬁlter and the PCM of a robustrecursive state estimator, under the situation that the data loss process is described by a Markov chain. It has been made clear that when the associated plant is both controllable and observ-able, this random matrix process converges exponentially to a stationary process that does not depend on its initial value. Moreover, when these state estimators start from the stabilizingsolution of the algebraic Riccati equation deﬁned by the system parameters of the associated plant, it is shown that this random matrix process becomes ergodic. An important observation is that when the data arrival probability is approximately equal to 1, the distribution of the corresponding stationary processcan be well approximated by a set of delta functions. Based on these results, two approximations have been derived for their stationary distributions, together with an error bound forone of these two approximations. It has been made clear that replacement of the update gain matrix of these estimators by a constant matrix usually sacriﬁce steady estimation accuracy,even if the measurement arrival probability is close to 1. Numerical simulations show that these approximations usually have a high accuracy.
